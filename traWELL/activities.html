Safaris

Sri Lanka's amazing nature reserves provide an unmatched sight of raw Mother Nature. They have one of the most diverse species populations on the Asian continent. A safari in one of these parks, particularly Yala and Minneriya, will provide you with an insight into the untouched species and their environments. It will be a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

Sri Lanka's fourteen wildlife parks are home to 86 different species of mammals, birds, and reptiles. The main attraction is large herds of wild elephants and the main predatory animal, the leopard.

Price:
-Toyota & Mitsubishi jeeps:

LKR 7000

-Mahendran & TATA jeeps:

LKR 6000

Time:
2-6 hours

Scuba Diving

There are several Scuba Diving locations in Sri Lanka, which are spread out over the island's Western, Southern, and Eastern coasts. These locations are so diverse and beautiful that both beginners and experienced scuba divers can enjoy the chances provided.

Scuba diving in Sri Lanka provides opportunity for everyone to enjoy wreck diving, reef exploration, and the variety of marine life. Scuba diving is available all year in Sri Lanka.

Price:
LKR 5000 upwards (depending on the place and depth)
Time:
2-8 hours/day

White Water Rafting

White Water Rafting is a physically demanding recreational activity as well as an exciting water sport. However, contrary to popular assumption, White Water Rafting is not a physically demanding activity. A moderate level of physical fitness and a positive attitude would do.

White Water Rafting, which is often experienced in white-waters as well as other turbulent streams, provides water sport enthusiasts with an unforgettable experience. Though the ability to swim is a definite benefit, a mandatory Personal Flotation Device and necessary gear for White Water Rafting would be given since it will save you from drowning if you go overboard.

Price:
LKR 1500 per person
Time:
90 mins (5 rapids at kitulgala)

Surfing

Despite the fact that it is not popular among Sri Lankans, surfing is one of the most popular activities in the country among international tourists. Surfing has been practiced in Sri Lanka since the early 1960s. Arugam Bay, Hikkaduwa, Weligama, Mirissa, Ahangama, and Unawatuna are renowned surf areas in Sri Lanka and among visitors.

Price:
-Renting a Board:

LKR 500 (half a day)
LKR 1000 (a day)

-Surfing lessons:

LKR 2000 (1.5 hour session)

Time:
You can surf for as long as you want.
