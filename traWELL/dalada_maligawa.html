SRI DALADA MALIGAWA

HISTORY

The Sacred city of Kandy is the final location of the Sacred Tooth Relic of Gautama Buddha. During the reign of King Keerthi Sri Meghavarna, Princess Hemamala and Prince Dantha brought the sacred relic to Sri Lanka from the ancient Indian city of Kalinga (Kithsirimevan 301 -328). It became a symbol of Sri Lankan rulers and was carefully kept in an unique temple established within the royal palace grounds, wherever the capital was located.

Ruins of such structures may still be seen at the ancient capitals of Anuradapura, Pollonnaruwa, Dambadeniya, Yapahuwa, Kurunegala, Kotte, and Gampola. While in Kandy, the last Kingdom, the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic remains to be the most revered shrine in the Buddhist world.

To this day, the Sacred Tooth Relic is being protected and religious rituals are being performed without pause under the strict supervision of the three chief custodians of the Tooth Relic, the most Venerable Mahanayake theros of Malwatta and Asgiriya chapters, and the lay custodian, the Diyawadana Nilame.

ANCIENT INFRASTRUCTURE

The temple, which is very important to Buddhists all around the world, also has enormous cultural importance. The building is in the distinctive Kandyan architectural style, with a blend of the unique style utilized to create "Dalada Mandira," the temples that formerly held the Sacred Tooth Relic in other kingdoms.

The shrine of the Sacred Tooth Relic is located in Kandy, near the ancient Royal Palace to the north and the forest reserve known as "Udawaththa Kelaya" to the east. Kandy Lake also known as "Kiri Muhuda" to the south and "Natha & Paththini Devala".

Inside the premises, there is a lot of exquisite work done in stone, ivory, and wood, some repaired and some as ancient as the temple itself. There's a lot of gold paneling as well. The temple also features a golden canopy that can be seen from miles.

NAVIGATION

A map is placed on the entry walkway to assist you with your navigation. However, most people simply go right up to the shrine room. On the walk in, look for the Madduma Bandara monument, a memorial of "that lion-hearted child hero" who has been immortalized in folk memory for his bravery in the face of death at the hands of his captors in 1814.

The stairs leading into the building cross a moat.
