Wildlife Attractions

Sri Lanka's wildlife is a nature lover's perfect holiday destination. There are several wildlife conversations, national parks, sanctuaries, and jungles to explore in Sri Lanka's tropics.

Visit the magnificent Sinharaja forest, Sri Lanka's only rainforest and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the wilderness of Yala National Park to see the rare leopard and the uncommon Sri Lankan Sloth Bear, as well as many other unique and non-endemic animals.

  • Yala National Park
  • Hikkaduwa Coral Reef
  • Minneriya National Park
  • Wilpattu National Park
  • Hortan Plains National Park

Beaches

Sri Lanka is a tropical island that is located in the deep blue waters of the Indian Ocean and the Bay of Bengal. The country has over a thousand miles of stunning golden beaches lined with coconut trees, making it a great place for a beach vacation. A huge number of premium resorts and villas, as well as cheap hotels, are located along the coast to provide travelers with some of the best beach vacations in Sri Lanka.

The beaches are obviously the main draw for both tourists and residents, but some towns, such as Galle on the south coast, also have historical legacy, which is an added bonus to a sun and sand vacation.

  • Mount Lavinia
  • Arugam Bay
  • Mirissa's Coast

Activities

Day trips and excursions in Sri Lanka include, but are not limited to, jungle hiking, diving and snorkeling, safaris, water-based sports such as white-water rafting and canoeing, hot-air ballooning, and many more options. Sri Lanka day tours provide a wide range of excursions to suit a variety of budgets, interests, and group sizes. Some trips require you to join a group and make new acquaintances, whilst others may be organized alone.

There is so much to pick from! Choose from wildlife safaris, city tours, cultural discoveries, and thrilling experiences when planning your perfect day excursions in Sri Lanka.

  • Surfing
  • Safaris
  • Scuba Diving
  • White Water Rafting

Heritage Locations

Explore Sri Lanka's Ancient Cities and traditions. Exquisitely carved stone monuments, beautiful statues of Lord Buddha, brilliantly decorated temples constructed into rocky overhangs, and irrigation feats that still surprise the globe are just some of the riches left by a proud civilization that dates back more than two thousand years.

  1. Sigiriya
  2. Temple of the Tooth
  3. Dambulla Cave Temple

Food

Sri Lanka is a tropical island country in the Indian Ocean that neighbors India. Therefore, India has had a considerable impact on Sri Lankan cuisine. However, when compared to Indian food, Sri Lankan cuisine has a distinct flavor.

Sri Lankan cuisine has a rich flavor and numerous spicy scents. The majority of Sri Lankans eat rice, coconut meat, corn, and cassava as their primary foods. They favor coconut milk and peppers, which are essential ingredients in practically every dish.
