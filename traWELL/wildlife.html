Yala National Park

Yala National Park spans both the Southern and Uva provinces of Sri Lanka, covering an area of about 97,878 hectares. Sri Lanka's best ecotourism location is around 250 kilometers (155 miles) southeast of Colombo. Safari rides are available in the park from private parties and the Department of Wildlife, and there are eco-friendly motels nearby to make your stay more peaceful.

The wildlife officials usually emphasize avoiding feeding animals and not taking up close photographs that upset the wildlife since it might affect both you and the animals in the park. Yala has one of the greatest leopard populations in the world, with approximately 25 individual leopards reported to exist in Block I of Yala. However, if you disturb these leopards' peaceful dispositions, it will be difficult to locate these creatures because they dodge the human sight.

Hikkaduwa Coral Reef

The Hikkaduwa coral reef is a network of fringing reefs around the island's southern tip, notably near the city of Hikkaduwa. Hikkaduwa beach is a popular location for both residents and visitors, and the reef is a treat to watch. The typical depth of the reef is around 16 feet, which is ideal for coral growth since it contains photosynthetic algae.

The best time to explore Hikkaduwa coral reef is when the water is clear and there is less possibility of storms, that is between April and November. Snorkeling and dive trips are the most popular activities here, while one can also charter glass-bottomed boats. However, travelers must be responsible enough to respect the reef and its species.

Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park is located in Sri Lanka's North Central Province, 182 kilometers from Colombo. The Minneriya National Park is centered around the huge reconstructed historical Minneriya Rainwater Reservoir, which irrigates a large portion of the district of Polonnaruwa.

Minneriya National Park, which is part of the elephant corridor that connects Kaudulla and Wasgamuwa National Parks, provides visitors with the opportunity to witness elephant herds all year. The greatest time to visit Minneriya National Park is from May to October, when the world-famous Great Elephant Gathering of Asian wild elephants takes place.

Wilpattu National Park

The park is the largest in the country, covering around 131,690 hectares (1300 square kilometers). Wilpattu gets its name from a large complex of roughly sixty wetlands known as 'Villu', which is not found in any of Sri Lanka's other national parks. Villus are small natural lakes that collect rainfall during the rainy season and provide habitat for species during the dry season.

There are several animal species to be found here, including a diverse range of mammals, birds, and reptiles. The animals are spread out thinly due to the park's size. The best time to visit Wilpattu is during the dry season, which falls between February and October. However the park is open throughout the year for visitors.

Hortan Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park is one of Sri Lanka's smallest national parks. Despite its size, it is one of the most beautiful parks in Sri Lanka. It is also one of the few national parks in Sri Lanka that allows people to walk. Horton Plains National Park, located in the Nuwara Eliya District of the Central Province, has the greatest altitude of any national park in Sri Lanka. It measures 2,100-2,300 meters (6,900-7,500 feet).

This area is high in biodiversity. There are several species there, many of which are endemic not just to Sri Lanka but especially to this particular park. It is another major factor, aside from the magnificent scenery for its popularity.
